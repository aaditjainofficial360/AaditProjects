Meta Platforms, Inc., also known as Meta, is an American multinational technology company that owns and operates various social media applications and services, such as Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and Oculus. The company was founded by Mark Zuckerberg and his college friends in 2004 as TheFacebook, Inc., and later changed its name to Facebook, Inc., in 2005. In October 2021, the company announced that it would rebrand itself as Meta Platforms, Inc., to reflect its focus on building the metaverse, which is an integrated environment that links all of its products and services.  

Meta Platforms is one of the world's most valuable companies and among the ten largest publicly traded corporations in the United States. It is considered one of the Big Five American information technology companies, alongside Alphabet, Amazon, Apple, and Microsoft. The company's main source of revenue is advertising, which accounted for 97.5 percent of its revenue in 2022. The company also invests in hardware, such as virtual reality headsets, smart glasses, and smart displays.   

Meta Platforms has more than 71,000 employees worldwide and operates in various regions and markets. The company's mission is to connect people and interests across its technologies and help them make their world their own. The company also faces various challenges and controversies, such as data privacy issues, content moderation policies, antitrust lawsuits, and social impact concerns.
